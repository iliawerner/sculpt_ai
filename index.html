Frequently Asked Questions

Do you work with RAG?

Yes, RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) is one of the key technologies in our AI infrastructure.

In practice, this solves the "smart" corporate search problem. Instead of spending hours searching knowledge bases, an employee asks a question in natural language—for example, "What are the requirements for a new developer?"—and instantly gets a precise answer with a link to the source. The system fully respects access rights, so employees only see the information they are permitted to view.

Which specific LLMs do you use?

We take a pragmatic approach: we choose the best model for the specific task and the client's security requirements.

Our infrastructure allows for three modes of operation:

  • Secure access to external LLMs: You get the power of the world's leading models (GPT-4o, Gemini, Claude), while your sensitive data never leaves your perimeter thanks to de-identification.
  • Managed cloud (Sculpt Cloud): We deploy a private cluster with powerful models in an isolated cloud container.
  • On-premise: For maximum security, we deploy the entire LLM infrastructure directly on your servers.

This approach allows us to not depend on a single model but to offer the optimal solution that meets your performance and security needs right here, right now.

Can you provide design services?

No, we are not a design studio. Our product is AI systems that make your design team an order of magnitude more effective.

We don't create designs ourselves; we implement AI solutions that take the routine off your specialists and speed up processes. For example, we can automate design system compliance reviews, reducing them from days to 15 minutes, as in the Avito case. Our goal is not to replace your designers but to give them the tools for exponential growth in speed and quality.

How do you create AI agents and automations?

We don't start with code; we start with the client's business process.

Our task is to understand where the team is losing time and money. We map the process (e.g., design review or content creation), find the bottlenecks, and only then design an AI agent or pipeline to automate them.

This approach ensures we are not selling "features" but a measurable economic impact: saved hours of expensive specialists and accelerated results. Depending on performance and security requirements, the technical implementation can be in Python or Java.
